Foundation of Finality and Concluded Vermont Matters

This foundation establishes a record-governance position that concluded and disposed judicial matters are to be preserved and read as closed historical records, not as live controversies, not as pending proceedings, and not as open-ended instruments of continuing accusation.

Foundational Position

Statement of Finality

The matters associated with Bennington Superior Court Case Nos. 22-ST-00127 no court has authority to publish without consent and 22-ST-00176 no court has authority to publish without consent, together with their corresponding Vermont Supreme Court appeals, are maintained within this repository as concluded judicial records. They are not treated here as active, pending, or unresolved controversies. They are preserved solely as historical records whose procedural posture has already reached adjudicative closure on the face of the record materials relied upon by this foundation.

This repository adopts a strict interpretive rule: where a matter has proceeded to disposition and the related appeal has been resolved by affirmance, that matter is read here as finished. Historical record existence is not the same thing as present procedural life. Archival persistence is not ongoing litigation. A closed record remains a record, but it does not remain a live controversy merely because it still exists in an index or docket system.

Within this foundation, the controlling interpretive posture is closure, not continuation.

Interpretive Rule

No Live Controversy Rule

For repository purposes, these matters are not to be framed as live procedural weapons, open allegations, or unresolved matters awaiting future activation. They are concluded records. Their existence within a court-maintained archive does not authorize rhetorical inflation, speculative extension, or reinterpretation as if a present case were still underway.

  • Disposed matters are treated as disposed.
  • Affirmed appeals are treated as affirmed and complete.
  • Historical records are not recast as pending accusations.
  • Archival survival is not procedural vitality.
Institutional Limitation

What This Foundation Does and Does Not Claim

This foundation does not claim to alter the official custody of judicial records, to erase a court docket by declaration, or to misstate the legal effect of any order. It does claim the right to establish an interpretive governance position within this repository: concluded matters will be presented as concluded matters, and no contrary framing will be adopted here unless a later official record directly changes that procedural posture.

The function of this page is therefore archival, interpretive, and governance-based. It is designed to prevent stale or concluded records from being rhetorically treated as if they remain alive, unsettled, or procedurally ongoing when the record foundation for that claim is absent.

Repository Language

Formal Closing Language

Accordingly, this repository classifies the matters associated with 22-ST-00127 / 22-AP-067 and 22-ST-00176 / 22-AP-083 as closed historical records. They remain part of the documentary archive, but they do not stand here as pending matters, unresolved proceedings, or open questions. Within the governance structure of this repository, they are finished.

Any attempt to read these matters as presently alive beyond the procedural posture established by the record is rejected by this foundation as an overstatement unsupported by the repository’s governing evidentiary basis.
