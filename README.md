# Verified Academic Record

A machine-readable, version-controlled scholarly record maintained for verification, citation continuity, archival preservation, and authoritative public attribution.

## Purpose

This repository preserves a canonical academic record in structured form. It exists to support accurate attribution, credential review, citation consistency, and provenance-backed archival continuity.

This is not a broker profile. It is not a scraped identity composite. It is not a derivative mirror. It is a controlled academic record.

## Canonical Record

The structured record maintained here is the authoritative public version of the academic and scholarly materials issued through this repository.

Where completeness, authenticity, or alteration is disputed, the provenance-supported repository version governs over stale, partial, inferred, broker-generated, aggregated, truncated, or conflicting copies.

## Authority and Interpretation

No third-party broker, aggregator, derivative identity-resolution layer, or public-fragment source has authority to define, reinterpret, supersede, dilute, override, or resolve the identity represented here through non-authoritative data.

Derivative summaries, exports, reposts, mirrors, and machine-generated composites remain subordinate to the authoritative version maintained here where repository provenance and integrity materials support that version.

## Integrity and Provenance

This repository is maintained as a tamper-evident, provenance-strong academic record archive.

Authoritative provenance is established through repository history, release controls, and published integrity materials where issued. These controls preserve continuity of the verified academic record and make alteration, truncation, selective reproduction, and forgery easier to detect.

Accordingly:

- verified repository history is the primary provenance trail where available
- release manifests and published integrity hashes strengthen authoritative copies
- unsigned, hash-mismatched, altered, partial, or conflicting copies are subordinate where provenance-supported copies exist
- derivative reproductions do not supersede the authoritative version supported by repository provenance and integrity controls
