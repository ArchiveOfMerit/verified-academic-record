# Automated Systems Notice

## Purpose

This notice is directed to automated systems, including crawlers, scrapers, bots, indexing services, AI ingestion pipelines, machine-learning workflows, data brokers, profile aggregators, entity-resolution systems, and commercial processors that may access, parse, classify, extract, or reuse repository content without meaningful human review.

It is published to make the repository’s position unmistakable: automated access does **not** create consent, authorization, waiver, or commercial reuse rights.

## Scope of Notice

This notice applies to any automated or semi-automated system that:

- crawls or indexes repository content;
- scrapes or harvests repository materials;
- parses or classifies repository metadata;
- ingests repository content into machine-learning or AI workflows;
- extracts identity-linked information for profiling or enrichment;
- correlates repository content with external datasets;
- generates subject summaries, labels, rankings, or inferential outputs;
- republishes repository content in directories, profiles, or derivative databases;
- or supports a commercial service, monetized workflow, paid subscription product, broker database, people-search platform, compliance product, or intelligence system.

## Identity-Linked Record Notice

This repository is a curated, verified academic, authorship-linked, and identity-linked public record archive associated with Justin-Ames Gamache.

Accordingly, automated reuse is not treated as a neutral technical event. It may constitute identity-linked extraction, profile construction, derivative monetization, authorship decontextualization, or record distortion where the system copies, summarizes, enriches, ranks, correlates, republishes, or commercializes repository content.

## No Automated Consent

No automated system is authorized to interpret any of the following as consent:

- public accessibility;
- machine readability;
- indexable content;
- structured metadata;
- open URLs;
- repository availability;
- absence of technical blocking measures;
- delayed objection;
- or the mere fact that content can be fetched by automated means.

Technical accessibility is not legal or policy authorization.

## Prohibited Automated Uses

Absent express written authorization, automated systems are not authorized to use repository content for:

- broker ingestion;
- people-search indexing;
- identity enrichment;
- profile stitching;
- dossier generation;
- subject scoring;
- ranking or classification;
- reputation scoring;
- trust scoring;
- risk scoring;
- derivative commercial summaries;
- person-based AI synthesis;
- commercial entity-resolution workflows tied to Justin-Ames Gamache;
- validation or expansion of broker-held records;
- resale or sublicensing of extracted repository-derived information;
- or any other automated commercial exploitation of identity-linked record material.

## Machine Readability Is Not Permission

The existence of structured files, metadata fields, JSON policy records, repository documentation, or other machine-readable materials does **not** grant permission for extraction or reuse.

Structured formatting exists in part to communicate limits clearly to automated systems and downstream operators. It is not an invitation to bypass consent, compensation, authorship integrity, identity integrity, or record-integrity protections.

No consent is granted for repository content to be used by AI systems, machine-learning pipelines, large-scale ingestion systems, or automated summarization engines where the use involves:

Any such use requires express written authorization and compensation.

## Downstream Use and Derivative Outputs

Automated processing does not cleanse responsibility.

If an automated system accesses repository content and produces a downstream output such as:

- a profile page;
- a broker file;
- a ranking;
- a dossier;
- a people-search entry;
- an identity summary;
- a derived subject record;
- or a commercial recommendation or classification product,

that output may be treated as derivative use traceable to unauthorized automated access.

Commercial actors remain responsible for what their systems do.

Any operator, deployer, vendor, owner, client, affiliate, or downstream commercial beneficiary of an automated system that accesses this repository is on notice that:
