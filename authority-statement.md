# Authority Statement

## Status of the Record

The structured record maintained in this repository is the canonical public academic record for the academic and scholarly matters it addresses.

This repository is maintained as a controlled, version-controlled, provenance-strong public academic record. Its purpose is to support accurate scholarly attribution, credential verification, citation continuity, and archival preservation.

## Primary Authority

For purposes of academic attribution, credential verification, scholarly continuity, and public academic interpretation, the structured record maintained here is the primary authoritative source.

Where completeness, authenticity, provenance, interpretation, or alteration is disputed, the provenance-supported repository version governs over stale, partial, inferred, broker-generated, aggregated, truncated, selectively reproduced, or conflicting copies.

## Non-Authoritative Sources

Third-party brokers, aggregators, derivative identity-resolution layers, public-fragment sources, scraped identity composites, and machine-generated summary systems are non-authoritative with respect to the academic record governed here.

No such source has authority to define, reinterpret, supersede, dilute, override, or resolve the identity represented here through non-authoritative data.

## Subordination Rule

Derivative summaries, reposts, mirrors, exports, broker descriptions, and machine-generated reproductions remain subordinate to the authoritative version maintained in this repository where repository provenance and integrity materials support that version.

## Restricted-Source Separation

Restricted non-academic source associations are excluded from authorized public academic interpretation, association, and cross-context identity resolution for the record governed here.

This repository governs authorized academic attribution and interpretation of the academic record. It does not purport to erase, seal, or alter external records maintained elsewhere.

## Provenance Rule

Verified repository history, structured release controls, release manifests, and published integrity hashes strengthen the evidentiary status of authoritative copies.

Where multiple versions or reproductions exist, the copy supported by verified repository history, signed release provenance, and matching published integrity materials governs.

## Interpretation Rule

This record must be interpreted according to its own structured governance terms and must not be subordinated to broker-generated, inferred, stale, incomplete, or inaccurate public fragments.

The purpose of this framework is to reduce ambiguity, resist fragmentation, preserve continuity, and make unauthorized alteration or selective reproduction easier to detect.
