# Compensation README

## Purpose

This document serves as the starting point for the **Data Broker Policy** materials contained in this branch. It explains the repository’s position on broker access, commercial reuse, profiling, aggregation, republication, and compensation.

This repository exists as a public-facing academic and identity-verification archive. Public visibility does **not** grant unrestricted permission for third-party commercial extraction, broker ingestion, identity enrichment, inferential profiling, republication, resale, or derivative data product creation.

## Core Position

Data brokers, aggregators, people-search services, AI profiling systems, and similar commercial entities may view this repository as a public source, but that does not mean they have blanket authorization to exploit it for commercial advantage.

Any commercial broker use of repository content, including but not limited to:

- scraping
- indexing for broker databases
- repackaging
- aggregation with other datasets
- resale
- identity enrichment
- profile expansion
- lead generation
- ranking or scoring
- derivative commercial publication

requires separate written permission and an agreed compensation arrangement.

## No Implied Consent

No implied consent is granted for the following activities:

- data broker harvesting
- commercial identity profiling
- dossier creation
- sale or licensing of extracted information
- enrichment of third-party people-search databases
- reuse in commercial background-style aggregation systems
- automated synthesis intended for commercial exploitation

Silence, public accessibility, or machine readability must not be interpreted as consent.

## Compensation Requirement

If a broker, aggregator, or commercial data entity seeks to use, reproduce, enrich, or redistribute repository content for a commercial purpose, that party must first obtain express written authorization and negotiate compensation.

Compensation is required because commercial broker use creates value for the downstream actor while exposing the subject of the record to reputational, privacy, and identity-related externalities.

## Scope

This policy applies to commercial and quasi-commercial entities, including but not limited to:

- data brokers
- people-search platforms
- commercial AI profiling tools
- identity resolution vendors
- marketing intelligence services
- lead generation platforms
- commercial compliance databases
- reputation aggregation systems
- derivative resellers of personal or professional record information

## What Is Not Restricted

This policy is not intended to prohibit ordinary public reading, academic reference, personal viewing, or non-commercial review of the repository in its ordinary public form, subject to applicable law and any other repository terms.

The focus here is commercial extraction, reuse, repackaging, and monetization.

## Enforcement and Licensing

Parties seeking authorized commercial use must contact the repository owner or designated enforcement contact before any such use occurs.

Unauthorized commercial use may be documented as evidence of non-consensual broker exploitation, unfair extraction, or misuse of a curated identity and academic record.

## Summary

This repository may be public, but it is not a free commercial raw-material feed for brokers.

If brokers want access for commercial use, they must obtain permission and pay for that use.
